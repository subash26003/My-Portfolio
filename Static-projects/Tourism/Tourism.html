Favourite place

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is an iconic white marble mausoleum built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. Completed in 1653, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of eternal love. The architectural masterpiece attracts millions of visitors annually with its stunning beauty.

GOLDEN TEMPLE

The Golden Temple, or Harmandir Sahib, is a sacred Sikh shrine in Amritsar, India. Completed in 1604, its gilded exterior symbolizes spiritual and temporal ideals. Surrounded by the sacred Amrit Sarovar, it attracts millions of pilgrims annually, fostering community and religious devotion. It embodies Sikh principles of equality, humility, and service.

MYSORE PLACE

Mysore Palace, located in Karnataka, India, is a stunning royal residence built in Indo-Saracenic style. Completed in 1912, it showcases intricate architecture, vibrant colors, and ornate interiors. The palace, also known as the Amba Vilas Palace, is a popular tourist attraction, offering a glimpse into the opulent heritage of the Wadiyar dynasty.

VARANASI TEMPLE

Varanasi, also known as Kashi, is renowned for the Kashi Vishwanath Temple. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, it is one of the holiest Hindu temples. Situated on the banks of the Ganges River, the temple attracts pilgrims and tourists seeking spiritual solace. Varanasi is a sacred city and a symbol of ancient Indian culture.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, a UNESCO World Heritage site located in Agra, India, is an iconic symbol of love and architectural brilliance. Commissioned in 1632 by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, the ivory-white marble mausoleum stands on the banks of the Yamuna River. Renowned for its exquisite Mughal architecture, the Taj Mahal is adorned with intricate carvings, geometric patterns, and calligraphy. Its central dome and four minarets showcase a perfect harmony of Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles.

Varanasi Temple

Sri Kashi Vishwanath Temple also known as the Golden Temple, it is dedicated to Lord Shiva, the presiding deity of the city. Varanasi is Said to be the point at which the first jyotirlinga, the fiery pillar of light by which shiva manifested has supremacy over others gods, broke through the Earth’s crust and flared towards the heavens. More than the Gaths and even the Ganga, the Shivalinga installed in the temple remains the devotional focus of Varanasi.
